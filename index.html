How we're different from typical movers

Three things matter most to our customers:

  • Direct service. The person you talk to on the phone is the person managing your move — not a broker reselling your job to whoever's available.
  • Transparent pricing. One all-inclusive hourly rate. No surprise charges for stairs, parking, wrapping, or "long carry" fees.
  • Same crew, every move. Background-checked, full-time Rainy Day employees with a lead foreman on every job.
Heads up about brokers. Many "moving companies" online are actually brokers — they take your money, then sell your job to whoever's available, often keeping 30–40% of the fee. With Rainy Day Movers you talk directly to the team doing the work. No middlemen, no mystery crews.

When to book

Weekends and the last week of the month book up fastest. As a rough guide:

  • Local moves: 1–2 weeks ahead is comfortable; same-week is sometimes possible depending on availability.
  • Long-distance & interstate: 2–4 weeks ahead is ideal — gives time for the onsite estimate and proper planning.
  • Commercial & office moves: 2 weeks comfortable, a month is ideal for larger or after-hours jobs.
  • Last-minute? Call us anyway — we're like firefighters for moving emergencies.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Answers to the questions we hear most. Don't see yours? Give us a call.

Do you provide estimates?
Certainly! We have a dedicated estimator. Let us know a time that works for you, and our manager will stop by to assess the move. If you're short on time or prefer, we can also provide an estimate via video.
Are you permitted to operate as a moving company?
Yes. To view our permit, visit utc.wa.gov/company/48515. Our WA UTC license number is HTG070086.
Is a truck included in the hourly rate?
Yes, a truck is included. Depending on the size of your move we dispatch 16', 24', or 26' trucks. For studios and 1-bedroom apartments a 16' truck usually does just fine; for bigger moves we bring a 26'.
Can you disassemble and reassemble my furniture?
Yes — and the service is free, included in your hourly rate.
My property requires a Certificate of Insurance (COI). Do you provide it?
We do, and we provide it for free. Please let us know 2–3 business days in advance and provide the HOA or property management name and address.
Do you provide last-minute moves?
Absolutely — depending on availability. Just give us a call. We're like firefighters for moving emergencies, minus the sirens.
How many movers should I get?
Studios usually need 2 movers. 2–3 bedrooms typically need 3 movers. Larger homes need 4–5. There are exceptions: a 6-bedroom house with few items might only need 2 movers, while a 1-bedroom packed with stuff could take 8 hours. We always match the crew to the actual workload.
What are your payment options?
We accept major credit cards, Venmo, Zelle, and cash.
Do you install TV mounts?
Our movers are licensed for moving only, so we cannot perform handyman tasks. We can carefully take your TV off the wall, but we cannot drill or install mounts. If you pre-install your TV mount, we'll gladly hang your TV safely for you.
Who actually does the moving?
Every move is completed by our own trained movers — not subcontractors. We hire, train, and manage all crew members ourselves to make sure your belongings are handled with care.
Are there any items you cannot move?
Yes — for safety reasons we can't transport hazardous materials (flammables, propane tanks, paint thinners, fireworks, ammunition), live plants on long-distance moves, perishable food, or pets. If you're unsure, just ask.
How long does a typical move take?
Studios run 3–5 hours, 1–2 bedrooms 5–8 hours, larger homes a full day. Big variables: distance between locations, parking proximity, stairs vs. elevator, building rules, and the actual amount of stuff. We share an estimate up front and only bill the time worked.
Do you offer packing services?
Yes — full or partial. See our packing page. We bring all materials and can pack just the kitchen, just the fragiles, or the entire home.
Do you provide blankets to cover furniture?
Yes — and you don't need to buy your own. Some customers without prior moving experience purchase blankets, shrink wrap, tape, even hand trucks. Please don't. We bring all of that, included in the rate.
Do you provide wardrobe boxes?
Yes — 5–7 wardrobe boxes per truck, free. If you have hanging clothes, we'll transfer them straight from your closet to a wardrobe box and back into the new closet, no folding required.
Can I ride in the moving truck?
Sorry — insurance says no. You'll need to drive separately or get a ride to the destination.
What if I underestimate how much stuff I have?
Don't worry — 97% of people do. We adapt on the spot, bring the right truck, and make it work without the drama.
Will you match other companies' prices?
We don't do "cheap." We do fair, honest, and worth it. If a competitor's quote looks impossibly low, it usually is — ask them what's not included.
Why should I choose Rainy Day Movers?
Because we're family-owned, local, and transparent. Sam and his wife run the company — she leads the team, he handles operations. Every customer gets reliable, hands-on service. Our growth speaks for itself.
Do you have a deposit requirement?
No large upfront deposit like brokers ask for. For long-distance moves we may collect a small reservation fee that's applied to the final bill. Final payment is due at completion of the move.
What happens if it's raining on move day?
It's Seattle — we expect it. We bring extra blankets and shrink wrap to keep your items dry between the door and the truck, plus floor protection so we don't track water through your home. The move doesn't stop for weather.
Are you insured if something gets damaged?
Yes. We carry commercial cargo and liability insurance. In the very rare event of damage, we'll work with you on a fair resolution. The vast majority of our moves complete with zero issues — but the protection is there.
Can you move pianos or other heavy specialty items?
Yes. Upright pianos, baby grands, gun safes, gym equipment, large artwork, antiques — mention them when you request the quote so we plan crew size, equipment (dollies, straps), and any extra protection.
Do you move on weekends or evenings?
Yes. Weekends are our busiest days — book early. We also do early-morning and evening moves to fit around work schedules and building move-in/move-out windows.
Is tipping expected?
It's never required, but always appreciated. A common range is $20–$40 per mover for a half-day move, $40–$80 for a full day. Cash is best. The crew works hard.
