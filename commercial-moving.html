Office Moves That Don't Stop Your Business

A commercial move isn't just bigger — it's different. Hours of downtime cost real money, employees need their workstations exactly where they were, IT has to come back online, and your building has rules about elevators, loading docks, and after-hours access.

We've moved law offices, medical practices, dental clinics, tech startups, retail spaces, and small manufacturing shops across the Greater Seattle Area. Same in-house crew, same transparent pricing, scaled up for the way businesses move.

The most-requested option: Friday-evening load + Sunday-morning unload + reassembly. Your team leaves the old office Friday and walks into the new one Monday with their computer plugged in and their chair where they left it.

  • 🪑
    Furniture & cubicles

    Desks, sit-stand workstations, modular cubicle systems, conference tables, lounge seating. Disassembled, labeled by workstation, reassembled exactly where it belongs.

  • 💻

How a Commercial Move Works

1

Walk-Through

We visit both spaces, inventory furniture and equipment, confirm building rules and elevator/dock access, and identify any specialty handling.

2

Plan

You get a detailed move plan: timing, crew size, truck count, COI delivery to both buildings, and a labeled floor plan for the new space.

3

Move

After-hours or weekend execution. Workstations get color-coded labels matching the new floor plan so nothing ends up in the wrong office.

4

Setup

Furniture is reassembled, monitors and equipment placed, debris removed. Your team walks in Monday and gets to work.

Pricing

Smaller offices (1–10 workstations) are typically billed hourly, just like residential — same crew, blankets, supplies, and tools included. Larger relocations and multi-day moves are quoted as a flat project rate after we've walked both spaces. Either way you'll know the price before move day.

Plan ahead when you can. Commercial moves involve more coordination than residential. Two weeks notice is comfortable; a month is ideal. Last-minute commercial moves are possible but tend to cost more because of crew availability and after-hours premiums.

Commercial FAQs

Common questions

Can you move our office after hours or on weekends?
Yes — most office moves happen evenings or weekends so the business is back up and running by Monday morning. We work around your schedule.
Do you handle IT equipment, monitors, and servers?
Yes. Monitors, computers, printers, A/V gear, and small server racks are wrapped, labeled, and transported with care. We coordinate with your IT team for disconnection and reconnection.
Can you move our cubicles and modular furniture?
Yes. Cubicle systems, sit-stand desks, conference tables, and modular furniture get disassembled, labeled, and reassembled at the new space.
What about confidential files and document storage?
Files stay in their cabinets when possible (we move the cabinet itself, faster and more secure). For loose files, we provide secure file boxes and maintain chain-of-custody from origin to destination.
Do you provide a Certificate of Insurance for our building?
Yes, free. Both buildings (origin and destination) usually require COIs — give us 2–3 business days notice with each property management company's address and we'll handle it.
How is commercial pricing structured?
Smaller offices are billed hourly. Larger or multi-day relocations are quoted as a flat project rate after a walk-through of both spaces.
How much advance notice do you need?
Two weeks is comfortable, a month is ideal. We can sometimes accommodate last-minute commercial moves but it depends on crew availability and may incur after-hours premiums.
Do you do storage between move-out and move-in?
For shorter gaps (1–7 days) we can often hold items in our truck or coordinate short-term storage. Longer gaps — let us know and we'll find a solution.
