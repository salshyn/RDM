Property Type Matters More Than You'd Think

Two homes with identical square footage can have wildly different moving times — and the difference usually isn't the stuff inside, it's the building itself.

  • 🏢
    Apartments & condos

    Stairs, narrow hallways, elevator wait times, long walks from the truck to the door, and building-imposed move windows all add real time. A 4th-floor walk-up easily takes 1–2 hours longer than the same load in a single-family home. Elevator-share buildings can add another 30–60 min.

  • 🏘️
    Townhouses

    Multiple flights of interior stairs slow loading. Tight garages and shared driveways can mean the truck parks at the curb instead of right at the door. Plan for ~20–30% more time than a comparable house.

  • 🏠
    Single-family homes

    Usually the fastest — we can park right next to the house, often back the truck up to the garage and load straight from inside. No stairs to the truck, no elevator queue, no building manager. Same volume of stuff moves in noticeably less time.

  • 🚪
    High-rises & secured buildings

    COIs, freight elevator reservations, loading dock time slots, and security check-ins all need to be coordinated 2–3 days ahead. We handle the paperwork (free COI included), but the building's rules drive the schedule.

Practical takeaway: when comparing quotes, don't just compare hourly rates — make sure the estimator understood your building, not just your stuff. A cheap quote that ignores stairs and elevator wait time will end up costing you more on move day.

How Many Movers Do You Need?

The honest answer: it depends on the amount of stuff, not just the size of your home. As a general rule:

  • Studios: usually 2 movers
  • 2–3 bedrooms: typically 3 movers
  • Larger homes: 4–5 movers

Exceptions happen — a 6-bedroom house with few items might only need 2 movers, while a 1-bedroom packed with stuff could take a full crew of 3 for 8 hours. We always match the crew to the actual workload.

Local Moving FAQs

Common questions

Is the truck included in the hourly rate?
Yes. Truck, fuel, blankets, supplies, and the crew are all included. We dispatch a 16', 24', or 26' truck depending on your move size.
How long does a local move usually take?
Studios and small 1-bedrooms typically take 3–5 hours. 2–3 bedrooms run 5–8 hours. Larger homes can take a full day. The big variables: distance between locations, parking proximity, stairs vs. elevator, and the amount of stuff.
Do you offer last-minute moves?
When availability allows — yes. Even if the date is tight, give us a call. We'll do our best to make it work.
My building requires a Certificate of Insurance. Do you provide it?
Yes, free of charge. Let us know 2–3 business days in advance and provide the HOA or property management name and address.
Will you disassemble and reassemble my furniture?
Yes — and the service is included in your hourly rate. Beds, tables, IKEA shelving, you name it.
What payment methods do you accept?
Major credit cards, Venmo, Zelle, and cash.
