Why Brokers Are a Problem

Most moving "brokers" never touch your items. They advertise your move, take your payment, and then sell your job to another moving company — keeping 30–40% of your money for themselves. Often, you won't even know the name of the company actually moving your belongings. It's a messy system that's hard to trust, and it's why so many people dread long-distance moves.

We don't play that game. At Rainy Day Movers, you deal directly with the team doing the work. Every dollar goes toward your actual move, not someone else's profit. That means better communication, better service, and complete accountability.

How to spot a broker: They quote you over the phone without seeing your home, push for a deposit before move day, and won't tell you the name of the carrier. If any of that sounds familiar, you're talking to a broker — not a mover.

Onsite Estimates You Can Count On

What "Long Distance" Means

A long-distance move is generally any move over 400 miles — though the exact definition can vary. Common destinations from Seattle include:

  • Seattle ↔ Portland, Eugene, or anywhere in Oregon
  • Seattle ↔ Bay Area (San Francisco, Oakland, San Jose)
  • Seattle ↔ Los Angeles & San Diego
  • Seattle ↔ Boise, Salt Lake City, Denver
  • Seattle ↔ Phoenix, Las Vegas
  • Seattle ↔ Texas, the Midwest, the East Coast

We also do cross-country moves regularly — including 3+ bedroom homes delivered the very next day for shorter coast-to-coast routes.

Long Distance FAQs

Common questions

How is long-distance pricing different from local?
Local moves are billed hourly. Long-distance moves are quoted as a flat rate based on your inventory, the distance, and the destination access. You'll know the price before move day.
How long does delivery take?
Depends on distance. Seattle to Portland or the Bay Area can often be next-day or 2-day delivery. Cross-country can be 3–10 days depending on routing. We give you a specific delivery window — not "sometime next month."
Are you actually licensed for interstate moves?
Yes. We're licensed by the WA UTC (HTG070086) and operate properly registered, USDOT-compliant trucks. You can verify our permit at utc.wa.gov.
Do you require a deposit?
We don't require a large upfront deposit the way brokers do. Final payment is due at delivery, with payment options including major credit cards, Venmo, Zelle, and cash.
What if I don't know my exact destination yet?
No problem. We can quote based on your origin and a target destination, then update if things change. We also offer short-term storage between move-out and move-in if needed.
Can you handle large or unusual items?
Yes — pianos, gym equipment, large artwork, antiques, and fragile pieces. Mention them when you request a quote so we can plan crew size and packing materials.
